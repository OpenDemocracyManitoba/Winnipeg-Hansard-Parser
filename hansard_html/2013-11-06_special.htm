INTRODUCTION AND WELCOME OF GUESTS AND ANNOUNCEMENTS

Mayor Katz: We have two Pages today: We have Elizabeth Walker of Fort Richmond Collegiate, resides in the St. Norbert Ward and Katrina Chanas, from John Henderson Junior High School from the North Kildonan Ward. Welcome.

As I'm sure everyone knows, it’s National "Take our Kids to Work Day", and I certainly would like to recognize in attendance the children of many of our civic employees, and family and friends of Councillors, so welcome and happy to have you here today.

Good morning everyone, and welcome to this Organizational Meeting of Council. Today is a very special day. I would hope you would all join me in wishing Councillor Harvey Smith a very happy 77th birthday. Happy birthday Councillor Smith. Councillor Smith, I want you to know it’s not every day we bring in Andrea Bocelli to sing "Happy Birthday", so I hope you appreciated that.

We are here today to select members of standing policy committees, and the members of other committees, boards and commissions. As all Councillors know, municipal government provides services crucial to our citizens. Winnipegers call upon city services in case of emergency. We reach out to 311 to answer or assist with all sorts of day-to-day issues; from city trees, to hockey rinks, to animal services. By serving on committees, boards and commissions, we ensure the work of municipal government carries on. This is indeed important work. This Organizational Meeting marks the official beginning to another Council year. As we look ahead, we see that Winnipeg’s outlook is positive. Our economy is one of Canada's most diversified. Consider these economic indicators: over the next five years, Winnipeg's economy is expected to create 31,000 jobs. Our population continues to grow. House prices in Winnipeg have increased by 51%, over the last six years and still provide good value compared to other Canadian cities. We know from CMHC data that 2013 is on track to be the strongest year for housing starts in Winnipeg since the late 1980s. We’re taking steps to ensure that major city departments are running efficiently. We will continue to focus on delivering services that people expect, while making the best use of our resources. Council has accepted all the recommendations of the recent fire paramedic station audit and we must ensure that these recommendations are now implemented. It’s about delivering value for taxpayer dollars, providing facilities that meet the needs of our citizens and ensuring that our processes are fair, open and transparent. If we do these things, the City of Winnipeg has a promising future before it. The private sector is investing heavily in Winnipeg. We are making slow, but sure progress on our local streets, sidewalks and backlanes through our local street renewal reserve. This long term plan will provide sustainable funding for local streets and will allow us to take pride in the state of our roads. Our active transportation network is growing with nearly 400 kilometres of pathways; more than double what we had in 2006. We’re taking steps to ensure that aboriginal people, who account for 11% of our population, have opportunities in our city. We are transforming our community centres. For each of the past five years, we have invested approximately $10 million per year to renovate and improve community centres. Downtown, the sports hospitality and entertainment district, is starting to take shape and the number of people living downtown continues to grow. We're on the cusp of taking our place on the national and even international stage. The Canadian Museum for Human Rights and the “Journey to Churchill” at Assiniboine Park will draw people to Winnipeg in ways that we can't yet imagine. Although we have different points of view at times, we are all motivated to do what’s best for the City of Winnipeg. I would like to take this opportunity to thank all Councillors for their passion and their commitment, and look forward to working together to make the city an even better place to live, work, play and invest. With that, I confirm the following Executive Policy Committee appointments:

Motion No. 1

Moved by Councillor Nordman,

Seconded by Councillor Steen,

WHEREAS City Organization By-law 7100/97 requires that the Deputy Mayor must be a member of the Standing Policy Committee on Finance;

AND WHEREAS it would be beneficial to increase the flexibility of appointments to Committees;

AND WHEREAS at its annual Organizational Meeting, Council must elect the members of the Standing Committees and the members of all other Committees, Boards and Commissions;

THEREFORE BE IT RESOLVED that section 6(2) of the City Organization By-law 7100/97 be amended to allow for either the Deputy Mayor or Acting Deputy Mayor to be appointed to the Standing Policy Committee on Finance;

AND BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED that Council make the following appointments and referrals to the related Committees, Boards and Commissions:

Councillor Nordman: Yes, I would, thank you very much, Mr. Mayor. I'll start by saying, thank you for the confidence you have shown in me by appointing me to Executive Policy Committee, as that old Stealers Wheel song said, "stuck in the middle with you." I rise today to bring forward a motion on the appointments, but also attached to that in Motion 1 is a bit of a change to By-law 7100/1997. It deals, specifically, with the Finance Committee and the former phrasing of the Finance Committee attendees included the Chair, the Deputy Mayor and one person appointment by Council. We would like to amend that to say, “the Chair, the Deputy Mayor, or the Acting Deputy Mayor, and one person appointed by Council.” So, that’s what the intent of the by-law is, to keep us in compliance. So, other than that, I would like to, as printed on the Motion 1, recommend we accept the appointments as delivered. Thank you.

Mayor Katz: Thank you very much. Councillor Orlikow.

Councillor Orlikow: Thank you Mr. Mayor. Shuffling the bases around EPC like which was done here is really an example for me of another missed opportunity in the City of Winnipeg. It was an opportunity for the City of Winnipeg to say, yes, some things happened the last couple of years. We need to look at maybe changing up the function and the role and the makeup of EPC. That wasn’t done. There was an opportunity to change the make-up so it would be more representative of the gender, where people live, generally representative of our population. Again, this EPC doesn't do that. It actually makes the council less representative of EPC. It really was, I thought, one of those moments when we sit around and we talk about, we are going to change, we understand the issues of the past, and we need to make some changes and move forward. I see nothing about moving forward; I only see moving backwards on these selections. So again, generally, we all sit around this room and we coalesce together and we say, for the benefit of all, we are going to be moving forward together. Well, I…we did that before. I remember some people saying, I am going to join EPC because I believe it’s the right thing to do, that I can change from within. Fantastic if you do and I’ll commend you all if you do; however, I am one of those people that fool me once, shame on me, fool me twice, shame on me too. So I look at this as I can't go down the same path again. I can't go down the path where we have people say, I selected a CAO, because I was told to. I didn't have the conviction at that moment to say, you know what? This is wrong. I was told to by a party that I must vote this way. That again was wrong. We…people admit that. We made a mistake. So why are we going to…now, do I believe this new makeup, do I believe any actions to this point represent that change coming? Absolutely not. So, with deep regret I can’t support the motion….recorded vote.

Mayor Katz: Councillor Nordman to close:

Councillor Nordman: I think we can move forward. I can't say as I agree with my colleague to the right, which is curious, that he's on the right, but, anyway, that is another subject. I will place the motion for the vote. Thank you.

Mayor Katz: Okay, recorded vote. I'll call the question. All in favour?
